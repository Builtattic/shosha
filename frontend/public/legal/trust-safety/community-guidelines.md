**Shosha™**

**Effective Date: ****1****st**** June****, 2026**

Shosha™ is a community-driven reputation and accountability platform operated by Builtattic Private Limited. These Community Guidelines govern user participation, reporting behavior, media submissions, commentary, interactions, and platform conduct across all services, products, profiles, reports, communities, and associated features made available through the platform.

By accessing or using Shosha™, users agree to participate responsibly and comply with these Guidelines in addition to the platform’s Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, and applicable laws.

**1. Purpose of the Platform**

Shosha™ is designed to facilitate public discourse, commentary, reporting, interpretation, and community-driven reputation signaling relating to publicly referenced conduct, actions, events, statements, participation, and social behavior involving individuals, organizations, public figures, and communities.

The platform permits criticism, disagreement, commentary, satire, opinion, praise, public accountability discussions, and social interpretation within reasonable boundaries intended to preserve platform integrity and user safety.

Shosha™ is not a court of law, investigative authority, governmental institution, or definitive arbiter of factual truth. Reports, comments, alignments, opposition signals, credibility indicators, and reputation metrics may contain disputed claims, subjective interpretations, inaccuracies, satire, incomplete context, or user opinions.

Users remain solely responsible for the content and claims they submit to the platform.

**2. User Conduct**

Users may:

- submit reports regarding publicly referenced actions or conduct; 

- provide commentary, criticism, or reactions; 

- align with or oppose reports and profiles; 

- attach media, references, or supporting material; 

- participate in communities and discussions; and 

- link publicly accessible social identities for credibility enhancement purposes. 

Users must not:

- knowingly submit fabricated evidence or manipulated media; 

- impersonate another person, organization, or public figure; 

- engage in coordinated harassment, targeted abuse campaigns, or brigading; 

- publish doxxing material or private/confidential personal information; 

- threaten violence, incite unlawful conduct, or encourage self-harm; 

- distribute unlawful sexual content or exploitative material; 

- upload AI-generated or manipulated evidence intended to mislead users or moderators; 

- evade moderation actions, suspensions, credibility penalties, or bans; 

- use automated systems, bots, or artificial amplification methods to manipulate platform credibility, visibility, or reputation systems; or 

- misuse anonymous reporting mechanisms to intentionally deceive, defame, harass, or target individuals or communities. 

**3. Reports, Claims, and Reputation Signals**

Reports submitted to the platform represent user-generated claims, interpretations, commentary, or allegations and may not reflect independently verified factual determinations.

Users are expected to act responsibly when submitting reports, attaching evidence, referencing external material, or making accusations involving identifiable individuals or organizations.

Shosha™ may permit disputed, controversial, critical, unpopular, or unverified submissions where appropriate in the context of public discourse, provided such submissions do not violate platform rules, applicable law, moderation standards, or platform integrity policies.

The platform reserves the right to:

- annotate reports; 

- restrict visibility; 

- reduce credibility weighting; 

- remove media; 

- disable interactions; 

- apply moderation penalties; or 

- permanently remove reports or accounts 

where manipulation, abuse, unlawful conduct, fabricated evidence, malicious targeting, coordinated misinformation, or platform misuse is reasonably suspected.

**4. Public Figure and Community Profiles**

Certain profiles available on Shosha™ may reference public figures, organizations, communities, or widely recognized individuals using publicly accessible information and references.

Unless explicitly verified by the platform, such profiles are not represented as officially operated, endorsed, managed, or controlled by the referenced individual or entity.

Users may request profile ownership or verification through platform-designated verification processes and supporting documentation requirements.

**5. Media and Evidence Standards**

Users submitting reports are encouraged to provide contextual references, publicly accessible sources, media, or supporting information where reasonably available.

The following may result in immediate moderation action:

- fabricated screenshots or altered evidence; 

- misleading edits intended to deceive; 

- manipulated AI-generated evidence; 

- unlawful explicit content; 

- exploitative or abusive material involving minors; and 

- media uploaded solely to harass, intimidate, or threaten individuals. 

Shosha™ may use automated systems, moderation tooling, manual review processes, and credibility systems to detect manipulation, abuse, or platform misuse.

**6. Moderation and Enforcement**

Shosha™ reserves the right, at its sole discretion, to:

- review, restrict, remove, deprioritize, demonetize, annotate, suspend, or permanently ban any content, account, report, community, or interaction; 

- apply credibility penalties or trust restrictions; 

- limit visibility or engagement features; 

- cooperate with lawful legal requests or investigations where required under applicable law; and 

- take reasonable measures necessary to preserve platform integrity, user safety, legal compliance, and operational stability. 

Moderation decisions may consider:

- credibility history; 

- prior abuse patterns; 

- evidence quality; 

- manipulation indicators; 

- coordinated behavior; 

- platform safety concerns; and 

- legal or operational risk factors. 

**6A. Participation Limits and Integrity Controls**

- Shosha™ may impose platform participation limits, reporting thresholds, interaction restrictions, credibility-based rate limits, feature-access conditions, or integrity controls in order to reduce spam, coordinated manipulation, brigading, platform abuse, artificial amplification, or other forms of misuse.

- Certain participation capabilities, including reporting frequency, visibility privileges, engagement functionality, credibility weighting, or access to platform features, may vary based on factors including account verification status, trust indicators, moderation history, platform standing, authenticity signals, account age, identity verification status, abuse prevention systems, or other platform integrity considerations determined by Shosha™.

- The platform reserves the right to modify, expand, restrict, suspend, or revoke such participation privileges at its sole discretion where misuse, manipulation, or harmful platform behavior is reasonably suspected.

**7. Anonymous Reporting**

Shosha™ may permit anonymous or partially anonymous reporting features in certain contexts. Anonymous participation does not exempt users from platform rules, moderation review, legal obligations, or accountability mechanisms.

Platform administrators may retain internal visibility into account ownership, moderation history, verification status, and reporting activity for safety, abuse prevention, operational integrity, and legal compliance purposes.

**8. Appeals and Review**

Users may request moderation review, verification review, credibility reconsideration, or content reassessment through designated platform processes where available.

Submission of an appeal does not guarantee restoration, reinstatement, reversal, or removal of moderation actions.

**9. Changes to These Guidelines**

Shosha™ reserves the right to modify, expand, update, or revise these Community Guidelines at any time in response to platform development, operational requirements, legal obligations, safety concerns, or evolving moderation standards.

Continued use of the platform following such changes constitutes acceptance of the updated Guidelines.
